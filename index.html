What do I see when I look at L‘atelier des femmes peintres?
Could this woman in L‘atelier (...) be reading aloud to the group? Does this present a case for collective feminist teaching and learning, and the circulation of ideas?

La Femme Libre
The first socially conscious French feminist journal aimed at improving women‘s employment conditions and was written and published only by women. The names credited to the founder and director were Jeanne-Désirée Véret Gay and Marie-Reine Guindorf. Both were recent activists in the Workers‘ College of the Saint-Simonian movement which women broke away from after 1831, forming a range of feminist movements as a result. Apostolat des femmes ( A Call to Women) was the title of the first issue; with the female contributors passionately calling out to all women to join their emancipation cause.In their writing, the founders recognise that women are educated to believe in the conventions that oppress them, but urge them to join their cause; forming a collective in association, understanding, deep feeling, and an organisation „that allowed them to indulge in something big“

Did Philippe-Jacques paint this as a record of these rarely seen ladies in free autonomous activity, and this rare occurrence of nudity?

A tactic some women developed to access a nude figure was to draw themselves, using their own bodies as an available tool. Indeed, the genre of self-portraits by women artists is one that we can look towards at this time in art history for guidance. Women were already depicting them-selves, revealing curiosity through pictorial representation, exploring first hand their identities and reflecting on their place in the world. In these self-portraits we are faced with direct confrontations with women: in the presence of all their qualities and personas. You see, women are inventive, adaptable, savvy and resourceful. They have taken their disadvantage into their own hands, defying attitudes about feminine propriety, championing women as suitable subjects, and teaching themselves the skills they need to succeed.
